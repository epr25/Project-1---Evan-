Goliath Grouper

Scientific Name: Epinephelus itajara

Introduction

Imagine yourself scuba diving one day, and you come across a giant brownish yellow fish, likely longer than you.

Despite how big it may appear, the fish itself is quite harmless, and may even exhibit some curiosity towards you.

What you may have saw was a Goliath Grouper! Which is a large bony fish and the largest of the grouper species.

Quick Facts

  • Goliath Groupers can grow up to 8 feet and weigh up to 800 pounds

  • They can be found in coastal and nearshore waters that are located around manmade structures or muddy bottoms
  • They are opportunistic predators that mostly feed on slower bottom moving species, such as crabs
  • They are currently listed as a critically endangered species
