Basking Shark

Scientific Name: Cetorhinus maximus

Introduction

Picture yourself swimming above the deep ocean, when all of a sudden; you spot a giant shark fin. However, once you go underwater, there is a key feature that tells you everything is ok. A large extendable gaping mouth.

The animal is none other than a basking shark, which does not see humans as prey.

Quick Facts

  • The basking shark is the second largest shark, and can grow up to 40 feet and weigh up to 5 tons
  • It's mouth is used as a filter, as it feeds off of zooplankton: its main source of food
  • While the basking shark isn't directly dangerous to humans, their large size and rough skin can pose a risk to the safety of individuals
